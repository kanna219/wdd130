Client / Developer Team Project - Report

Client Report

I decided what content and images to use for my site plan by thinking about what best represents me and my interests. I chose simple and clear content so that visitors could easily understand the purpose of my website. For images, I selected ones that matched the theme and made the site visually appealing.

I communicated with my developer mainly through email and class discussions. There were no issue with communication.

My developer met my expectations for the site. The design and structure were close to what I had imagined, and I appreciated their effort in completing the project.

Next time, I would give clearer and more detailed instructions at the beginning. I think better communication and more specific feedback would help improve the final result.

Japan Complete

Lead Developer Report

I managed my team by dividing tasks between my junior developers and giving them instructions on what pages they needed to work on. I tried to check their progress regularly and support them when they had questions.

I communicated with my client through canvas messages. Sometimes it was difficult to fully understand the client's expectations, but I tried to ask follow-up questions to make sure I was on the right track.

I handled publishing the client's site using GitHub Pages and shared the link with the client once it was ready. This process helped me understand how to make a website accessible online.

Managing the site with GitHub was sometimes challenging, especially when dealing with commits and updates, but it was a valuable experience and helped me learn how version control works.

Junior Developer Report

I communicated with my team lead through email and class interactions. Sometimes there were small misunderstandings, but we were able to resolve them by asking for clarification.

I generally understood what was expected of me for each site, although there were times when instructions were not very detailed. In those cases, I tried to confirm the requirements before starting my work.

I was able to contribute to the sites by completing my assigned pages and making sure they matched the overall design.

I had some difficulties using GitHub, especially when adding and updating files, but I learned how to fix these issues over time.

General Experience and Reflections

The most challenging part of this project was communication and explaining my ideas clearly. Sometimes I found it difficult to express what I wanted or to understand others. However, I was able to improve by asking more questions and trying to communicate more actively.

I enjoyed working as part of a team and experiencing different roles. It was interesting to see how a project develops from planning to completion.

If I could do this project again, I would make a group chat to communicate more clearly and make sure I fully understand the requirements before starting. I would also try to improve my explanation skills.

After this experience, I feel more confident in working on real-world projects. I understand better how teamwork, communication, and tools like GitHub are used in actual development environments.
